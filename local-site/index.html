Morning: Natural Materials

Eko Rubber

Eko Rubber

Recycled rubber raw material sourced from unused rubber scraps and products. The resin is produced by a method called Advanced Hybrid Devulcanization Method that takes the processed rubber back into its pre-vulcanized state, ready again for processing. Some common applications of this material are in: Accessories, Consumer Electronics Casings, Footwear, Furniture, Safety Equipment, Sports Equipment, and Toys.

Balsa Tec

Balsa Tec

A modularly flexible composite panel made from balsa end grain for composite and sandwich construction. The material has good durability and excellent compressive strength. Some common applications of this material are in: Lamination, Metal Working Tools, WoodWorking Tools

PureTemp

PureTemp

Bio-based phase change material (PCM), which changes phase (e.g. melting or freezing) to absorb or release relatively large amounts of latent heat at a constant temperature. When the temperature becomes warmer than the freeze point of the PCM it will liquefy, absorbing and storing heat during the process. Conversely, when the temperature drops, the material will solidify and give off heat, warming the material coated or impregnated with PCM. This material has the capability to keep the temperature constant for an extended period of time. It is mostly applied in casting.

Elegance

Elegance

Flexible, light-weight translucent woven metal mesh for use in interior and exterior applications. Fine stainless steel filaments produce a horizontal mesh that can be folded or curved to suit. Some of its common applications are in: Die Cutting, Lamination, Metal Working Tools, Stitching, Welding

Alpolic

Alpolic

Highly reflective hard-anodized aluminum composite. This material is intended as an alternative to glass, tempered glass and acrylic for mirrored surfaces as it is thinner, lighter in weight, will not shatter or break, and is easy to fabricate and install. Additionally this smooth, rigid and durable composite has a higher conductivity than acrylic so it does not collect dust. Some of its common applications are in: Cold Pressing/Deep Drawing, Die Cutting, Metal Working Tools, Printing, WoodWorking Tools

Textrafine

Textrafine

Blanket insulation composed of 80% recovered long fiber, filament glass, with a 90% total recycled content. The insulation is manufactured by a unique process where long, textile-type glass fibers are bonded together in random orientation by a stable thermosetting resin, which produces a strong, resilient insulation that will return to full thickness following compression. Some of its common applications are in: Die Cutting, Lamination, Metal Working Tools, Stitching, WoodWorking Tools

Cementfoam

Cementfoam

Lightweight building material composed of 60% expanded polystyrene foam (EPS) and a 40% cement layer which is reinforced with a textile mesh. The material is insulating, flame retardant, moisture resistant, contains no VOCs, and is sound absorbing.

Filigree Electroplating: Metallized Carbon Fibers

Filigree Electroplating: Metallized Carbon Fibers

Metallized carbon fibers to create functional carbon fiber reinforced plastics (CFRP) as well as new fiber-reinforced metals for use in component monitoring, lightning and wear protection in aerospace, automotive and renewable energy sectors. Carbon fibers and CFRP are widely replacing metallic materials for their light-weighting benefits. Some of its common applications are in: Architectural Elements, Mass Transportation, Medical Devices, Safety Equipment

SITgrid Carbon

SITgrid Carbon

3D textile composed of top and bottom lattices of carbon fiber, polyester (PET) or polypropylene (PP) binding yarns, and a “pilewarp” of polyethylene (PE) monofilament to create the spacial structure. The advantage of carbon over other materials for concrete reinforcement, including conventional steel or glass fiber, is its superior resistance to alkalis and corrosion; this material makes it unnecessary to have a thick exterior concrete covering in order to protect the reinforcement from corrosion. It is mostly applied in casting.

TechnoGel

TechnoGel

A breathable, dimensionally stable, polyurethane-based gel that is shock absorbent and distributes pressure equally. The gel is flexible and elastic with good recovery properties. It does not contain plasticizers or other volatile components so it is stable and retains its properties over an extended time. It can be stamped or cut into shaped sheets, individually molded into shapes, directly laminated with decorative materials during manufacture, or bonded later with standard polyurethane glue. Some of its common applications are in: Die Cutting, and Lamination

Koroyd

Koroyd

An engineered thermoplastic honeycomb comprised of a co-extruded polycarbonate (PC) for energy absorption. This structure provides uniform mechanical properties due to its circular cell structure, and offers high compressive strength in a low-density material, decreasing transmitted force and peak g-force acceleration. The honeycomb is an efficient energy absorber, which is vital to impact protection, and is highly breathable. Some of its applications are in: Die Cutting, Lamination, Metal Working Tools, Thermoforming/Compression Molding, and WoodWorking Tools

Lumina Spark

Lumina Spark

Acrylic (PMMA) vertical surface interior architectural panels that transform single light sources from one edge to the entire surfaces within which thousands of points of light appear to seemingly float. This is created by light reflected against a back mirrored or colored surface and a variety of 'cuts' in the core section of the material, giving a depth effect. Some of its common applications are in: Lamination, Metal Working Tools, WoodWorking Tools
